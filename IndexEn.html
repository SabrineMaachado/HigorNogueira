Bertrand Husserl had been a precursor thinker of the philosophy of phenomenology and had a great influence on Heidegger's thought, even if he disagreed as to the direction and direction of his phenomenological investigation, since for him his analyzes had an ambiguous character, solving Heidegger's problem through of an attempt at categorical resolution and with a notion of identity that would refer to the notion of truth, resolving it from its own subject. This text explains one of the main differences between Husserl and Heidegger, regarding the phenomenological purpose that both have with their respective differences in phenomenological orientation, because although both start from the analysis of how the transcendental orientation of phenomenological investigation should be carried out, there will be differences in analysis. in the interpretative methods of their respective investigations.

For Husserl thinks that his transcendental problematic had no correlation whatsoever with a supposed ontological investigation, since in his view the transcendental would not find in the being of an entity, an opinion of which Heidegger will come to disagree, a stalemate that will only be solved by clarifying the way of being an entity, shifting the problem to a field of an ontological nature, with the need for an existential analysis of being by the German thinker. What is important to emphasize about the Heideggerian project in its first phase in search of the more comprehensive concept of being is the common establishment between the Cartesian metaphysical principle and its ontological project, since both affirm an inevitable existence of the condition of object as thought.
